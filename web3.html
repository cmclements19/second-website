Sports

Lacrosse

Lacrosse is Clay's most recent new found love in his life. He just started playing three years ago and felt apart of the lacrosse lifestyle from the start. He has always know he was Native American but never was able to actually get in touch with his heritage until he found lacrosse. To him, lacrosse is more than a game, it is called the "Medicine Game" in the Native communities because it is a healing game. When you play the Creator's game, it brings you one with Earth and the Creator. Which then ultimatly makes you feel at peace playing for your brothers and the Creator, but most importantly keeping the heritage alive. He hopes to always play the game until he dies and then pass on the game to his children so they can experience the Medicine Game and be one with the Creator.

Football

The sport of football was Clay's first sport he ever played. He grew to love it through middle school and especially loved playing tight-end and becoming the MVP of his team with the most points for the season. Once he was in highschool he stopped playing football and moved on to just watching college and pro football whenever he can. He loves watching his Green Bay Packers play and also his two favorite college teams, USC and Ohio State. Although he misses playing the sport, he is fine with just spectating because his heart is stuck on lacrosse.

Racing

The most relatively new passion of Clay's, is building cars. His first truck is a 1956 Ford F100 that he is still custom building to this day. From when he was born, he had a passion of cars passed on to him and he has turned it into a full-blow addiction. He cannot stop thinking about cars or what he would like to do to them to make them better looking and performing whenever he sees one he likes. There is a chance he may be able to persue his dream of building custom cars and truck in the near future and turn his passion into his career so he can play around with life size toys all the rest of his life and be able to share it with others. But most importantly, he would love to pass down his passion down to his kids so he can keep the family tradition going.
